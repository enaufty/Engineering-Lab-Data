# E2: EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN FOR INVESTIGATION OF COOLING OF SUGAR SOLUTION DEPENDING ON TIME

This repository contains a Python-based visualization tool designed to analyze and compare the cooling rates of liquids under various experimental conditions. The project focuses on how physical factors—such as convection, geometry, and solution composition—affect heat transfer dynamics.

## Project Overview

In this engineering laboratory study, we monitored the temperature decay of a liquid over time to evaluate the efficiency of thermal dissipation. By quantifying these cooling curves, we can better understand the variables that influence **Newton's Law of Cooling** in a controlled environment.

### Experimental Variables

The script visualizes four distinct experimental sets to demonstrate the sensitivity of the cooling process:

* **Set-1 (Reference):** The baseline cooling profile under standard ambient conditions without external interference.
* **Set-2 (Stirring Speed):** Investigates the role of **forced convection**. Higher stirring speeds increase the heat transfer coefficient, leading to a faster transition to thermal equilibrium.
* **Set-3 (Surface Area):** Evaluates the effect of **geometry**. Increasing the surface-area-to-volume ratio facilitates more rapid heat exchange with the surrounding environment.
* **Set-4 (Salt Solution):** Examines the impact of **solution composition**. The addition of solutes like salt alters the specific heat capacity and thermal conductivity, thereby modifying the cooling trajectory.
